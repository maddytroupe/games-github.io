Privacy Policy
Updated: January 22, 2025

MaddyTroupe ("we," "our," or "us") is committed to protecting your privacy. This Privacy Policy explains how your personal information is collected, used, and disclosed through our application, Aqua-Strike. By accessing or using Aqua-Strike, you confirm that you have read, understood, and agree to the terms of this Privacy Policy and our Terms of Service.

Key Definitions and Terms

  • No Data Collection: Aqua-Strike does not collect, store, or process any user data.
  • No Internet Connection: Aqua-Strike does not require an internet connection to function.
  • No External Servers: Aqua-Strike does not use any servers to store or process user data.
  • No Marketing or Advertising Use: Aqua-Strike does not utilize user data for marketing or advertising purposes.
  • No Data Sharing: Aqua-Strike does not share user data with any third party under any circumstances.
  • Built-In Security: Aqua-Strike is naturally secured by the Oculus platform’s built-in security settings.

Compliance and Audits

Aqua-Strike commits to:

  1. Annual Self-Certification: We certify our compliance with this Privacy Policy and all applicable terms and policies.
  2. Purpose and Use Verification: Aqua-Strike does not engage in data sharing. The inputs processed by the game are strictly limited to ensure compliance with this Privacy Policy.
  3. Audit Readiness: Aqua-Strike is open to audits by Meta or any third party authorized by Meta to confirm compliance with this policy.

Processed Inputs

Aqua-Strike only processes the following inputs:

  1. Headset Orientation and Position
  2. Hand Controller Position, Orientation, and Button/Sensor Inputs

No other inputs, such as microphone data, are processed by Aqua-Strike.

 

Stored Data

  • Aqua-Strike saves level completion status locally on the user’s device.
  • This data is stored on the device and is not transmitted to any external server or system by Aqua-Strike itself.
  • However, as part of the Oculus platform’s features, Meta may automatically back up this data to the cloud for syncing across devices. This backup is managed entirely by Meta and governed by Meta's privacy policies.
  • Users can remove this data by clearing the app cache.
